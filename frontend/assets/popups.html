Welcome to the Marsquake Rover project!

"I would like to die on Mars. Just not on impact."

The year is 2043, and we're looking to land on the fourth rock from the Sun in the near future. Buuuut, we need some serious planning for that.

Test out the Marsquake Rover simulator for a successful mission, and help out our little robot on the red planet!

Drag and drop

"Ignoring the signs is a good way to end up at the wrong destination."

The green flag is our rover's landing site, and we've found evidence of water at the red flag! (Cool stuff, right?)

Drag and drop any flag to test out several different locations for our source and destination.

Hills and valleys

"Obstacles. Most can be overcome by the appropriate use of explosives."

Something not at home can be something that's hostile. Mars is full of high hills and low valleys and all sorts of crazy terrains.

Left click and move your mouse over an area to add/remove obstacles. Our rover won't be able to move over these obstacles :(

Algorithms

"A word used by programmers when they don't want to explain what they did."

Open the side panel and explore 11 different algorithms that our rover can follow.

Note that some nice algorithms will always find the shortest path (like A*), and some not-so-nice algorithms will take a longer walk (like beam search).

Modes

"To be or not to be? I want to know where to be."

Multiple sources: Test out several possible landing sites by placing multiple sources.
Multiple destinations: Find out the nearest water reservoir by placing multiple destinations.
Checkpoints: Need a detour? Place checkpoints and our rover will visit them in order.

Right click to add or remove these extra flags from any mode. Note that our fine rover will find the nearest source-destination pair in the first two modes!

Configurations

"Variety is the spice of life. And algorithms as well."

Tinker around with different settings in our simulator. Note that some of these might be disabled based on the algorithm and other configurations.

Allow diagonals: Whether the rover is allowed to move diagonally.
Cut corners: Whether the rover is allowed to cut the corner of an obstacle.
Bidirectional: We lost a rover on Mars in the 2034 mission, and now the two rovers need to find each other!

Define sunlight

"A day without sunshine is like, you know, night."

Our rover runs on solar energy and would prefer walking on sunshine.

Click on define sunlight and move the slider to adjust intensity. Left click and move over any area to assign the chosen intensity.

Note that unweighted graph algorithms like jump point search will be unaffected by this setting.

Wormholes

"If I could teleport, I'd probably still be late."

One of our engineers is a big sci-fi fan, and hopes to find teleportation wormholes on Mars someday.

Sometimes our destination might be unreachable, but don't you worry! Play around with our wormhole feature to teleport from one place to another.

Random mazes

"So much of life is determined by pure randomness."

Sometimes the journey can be unpredictable and we need to prepare for that.

Select one of our maze generation algorithms and click on Random Maze to generate a random terrain.

Let's go!

"Shoot for the stars and you'll land on Mars."

Click on Find Route to get going, and Reset Grid to restore the default settings.

Go forth and conquer, Martian. Make us proud.
